Educational

Before a Fire:
Create an emergency plan with escape routes and meeting points.
Install and test smoke alarms and carbon monoxide detectors.
Keep fire extinguishers accessible and teach everyone how to use them.
Practice fire safety measures and keep flammable items away from heat sources.
Educate your family about fire safety and have an emergency kit ready.
During a fire:
Stay calm and follow your emergency plan.
Evacuate immediately and crawl low to avoid smoke.
Find an alternate exit if doors or handles are warm.
If trapped, stay in a room with closed doors, signal for help, and call 9-1-1.
After a fire:
Wait for authorization before re-entering your home.
Check on others who may need help.
Seek medical attention for injuries.
Contact authorities for temporary housing if needed.
Notify your insurance company and document damage.
Use protective gear when cleaning your property.
Discard contaminated food and water.
Follow safety guidelines for cleanup.
